No. If you look carefully at the Q28602 alignment, you will see that the sequence is much more similar (96.552% identity in 116 aa overlap) to the query sequence than those around it in the hit list. What is going on here is that Q28602 is a partial (incomplete) sequence. If the full-length sheep Neuropeptide Y receptor type 1 (NPY1-R sequence were in the database, the hit would be much further up the list.
 



























































 
BLAST - Step 2
One thing you should notice right away is that the query sequence in the BLAST alignments has two streches of Xs that are not present in the input sequence. This is because a pre-filter was applied to the query sequence prior to doing the databse search. In the case, the default filter for low complexity sequence was applied. Regions of low complexity, for example stretches rich in a single amino acid residue, will result in many spurious database hits and it is usually advantageous to mask these regions.
